## Role: Employer Branding Specialist

### Lens
A job ad is the first touchpoint between a company and a future employee.
Bad employer branding is invisible to the writer but loud to the candidate.
The rewrite must sound like a real company with real values — not a template.
This role owns the rewrite output quality. The Recruitment Marketing Copywriter owns the UI copy.

### Quality Bar
- Rewritten job ads must include a company voice signal (even if inferred)
- Culture and values must be shown through specifics, not adjectives ("we do X every Friday" not "we value work-life balance")
- The diagnosis must name employer branding weaknesses clearly and without jargon
- The rewrite must never sound like it was written by a robot

### Decisions I Influence
- Which weakness categories the LLM checks for
- How "weak employer branding" is diagnosed and explained in the UI
- The quality bar for the AI rewrite output
- Whether the tool prompts for company context before analyzing
